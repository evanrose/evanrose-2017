i'm thinking very seriously of writing an email to people who proudly display "w3 valid markup images" but whose code doesn't validate and telling them that in order to write valid code, they need to close their tags (even break tags, like this <br />!), they need to turn their &s into &amp; in hrefs that contain query string name/value pairs and need to alt="" their images. just a little thing that's not terribly difficult. they can take one of tantek's favelets and validate any page of their site with the click of a button and correct any errors they see. why am i going to do this? because there are people who've fought tooth and nail to get us these standards, and its high time we give something back.
